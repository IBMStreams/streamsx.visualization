Sharing READ Dashboards

Follow these steps to make your READ app and dashboards publicly accessible.
  • Uncheck the private button for your app in the apps tab.
  • Use a public facing IP address for your READ.share service. For example, suppose 8.74.12.82 is the public facing IP of the machine the READ.share service will run. Start your READ.share service using the command meteor --port 8.74.12.82:3002. Do not use http://localhost:3002.
  • In the apps tab for your app, within the field labeled READ.Sharing service URL, enter the URL where the READ.share service is running (http://8.74.12.82:3002 in our example) and click .
  • While creating URL based datasets within your READ app, use only publicly accessible URLs that return a JSON response. Do not use URLs relative to localhost or local IP addresses).
  • Use the public link for app generated by READ to share your READ app publicly.
